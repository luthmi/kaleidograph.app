An Instrument for Visualizing Harmony

A way to see what music sounds like.

The tone of the note controls color. Chord quality controls geometric behavior — different chords (major, minor, diminished, 7th) all have a unique symmetry. Consonance and dissonance shape complexity. The result is a visual language for harmony.

Most music visualizers react to waveforms — amplitude, frequency, beats. The Harmonic Kaleidograph is fundamentally different. It reads the actual harmonic content of the music: which notes are sounding, what intervals they form, how chords move and resolve. What you see is the real mathematical structure of the music, made visible.
